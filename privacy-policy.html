Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how personal data may be processed when you visit icaif2026.org, use the ICAIF '26 website, or contact the conference organizers. This notice is intended to provide the information required by Article 13 of Regulation (EU) 2016/679 (GDPR).

Last Updated

March 30, 2026

This notice reflects the current implementation of the ICAIF '26 website as reviewed on March 30, 2026. If the website adds new forms, tracking tools, registration features, or integrations, this notice should be updated accordingly.

Data Controller

For the purposes of this website, the data controller is the ICAIF '26 Organizing Committee, reachable at info@icaif26.org.

General conference contact details currently published on the website include Andrea Maurino.

Personal Data We May Process

Depending on how you use the website, the following categories of personal data may be processed:

  • • Browsing and technical data: IP address, date and time of the request, requested URL, browser and device information, referrer URL, and similar technical metadata generated when pages are loaded.
  • • Contact data you provide voluntarily: your name, email address, message content, attachments, and any other information you include when you contact the organizers by email.
  • • External platform data: if you follow links to third-party services such as Microsoft CMT, those services may collect and process personal data under their own privacy notices.

Purposes and Legal Bases

Purpose Legal Basis
Deliver website pages, maintain performance, protect the site, and prevent abuse or technical misuse. Legitimate interest under Article 6(1)(f) GDPR.
Respond to questions, conference-related requests, or organizational communications sent by email. Legitimate interest under Article 6(1)(f) GDPR and, where applicable, steps taken at your request before registration or participation under Article 6(1)(b) GDPR.
Comply with legal obligations, handle disputes, and protect legal rights. Article 6(1)(c) and/or Article 6(1)(f) GDPR, as applicable.

Third-Party Services and External Links

The website currently loads certain third-party technical resources directly from external providers. When pages are opened, your browser may connect to these providers, which may receive technical request data such as your IP address and browser metadata.

  • • Google Fonts via fonts.googleapis.com and fonts.gstatic.com for web fonts.
  • • Tailwind CDN via cdn.tailwindcss.com for CSS framework delivery.
  • • Lucide Icons via unpkg.com for icon loading.
  • • Microsoft CMT when users follow submission or proposal links published on the site.

These third-party providers act under their own privacy notices and terms. The ICAIF '26 website does not control the privacy practices of external sites and services reached via outbound links.

Cookies, Analytics, and Similar Technologies

Based on the current implementation reviewed on March 30, 2026, this website does not intentionally use first-party analytics tools, advertising tags, or profiling cookies.

The site is static and does not include registration forms, newsletter sign-ups, or user accounts. However, third-party providers used to deliver fonts, scripts, and linked external services may independently process technical request data under their own policies.

Recipients and International Transfers

Personal data may be made available, where necessary, to hosting and infrastructure providers, website maintenance providers, and third-party technical services involved in delivering the website. Data may also be disclosed where required by law or to protect legal rights.

Because this website uses third-party resources and external services that may operate globally, some technical data may be processed outside the European Economic Area. Where this occurs, the processing is governed by the privacy notice and transfer mechanisms adopted by the relevant provider.

Retention

Technical logs and browsing data are retained only for the period strictly necessary to ensure website operation, security, troubleshooting, and abuse prevention, unless a longer retention period is required by law or imposed by the relevant service provider.

Email correspondence is retained for the time necessary to handle the request, manage any follow-up, and meet organizational, legal, or defensive retention needs.

Your Rights

Under the GDPR, you may have the right to request access to your personal data, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, objection to processing, and, where applicable, data portability.

To exercise these rights, please contact the organizers at info@icaif26.org. You may also lodge a complaint with the Italian supervisory authority, the Garante per la protezione dei dati personali, if you believe that your data has been processed unlawfully.
